Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution

Biography

Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar (1891–1956) was an Indian jurist, economist, politician, and social reformer who campaigned against social discrimination toward the untouchables (Dalits), while also supporting the rights of women and workers.

He played a pivotal role in the drafting of the Indian Constitution and is often referred to as the "Father of the Indian Constitution."

Ambedkar was India's first Minister of Law and Justice and the chief architect of the Constitution of India. His efforts to eliminate social discrimination and uplift the marginalized sections of society continue to inspire generations.

He was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1990, posthumously.
